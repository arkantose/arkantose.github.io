🐾 How Fetch works

Two parts: how you talk in the headset, and the buttons here on your PC.

🎧 In the headset

Record the module like always. When you spot something, open with a trigger word, say the whole thought, and close with "Pause":

"Timestamp ... Pause" a specific fix tied to a spot. Say where it is, what's wrong, and what to do. Fetch attaches a clickable time so the contractor jumps straight to that moment in the video.

"Timestamp: the front intake panel should be brushed metal, not plastic. Pause."

"Sidenote ... Pause" a general note not tied to a moment. No time gets attached.

"Sidenote: the lighting feels darker than the other modules. Pause."

Tips: one thing per callout (three issues = three callouts). Say where it is out loud, since Fetch can't see where you were looking. Saying "Pause" at the end is what lets the module narrator keep playing, anything it says after your "Pause" is ignored. Just don't use the word "pause" inside a callout.

💻 On your PC

The tabs down the left side, roughly in the order you use them:

  1. Connect. Plug in the Quest, wait for the green dot, then pull your recordings. Ones you already pulled are checked and greyed so you don't grab them twice. The 🗑️ on a recording deletes it off the headset and out of your Library. Anything you already published stays safe in History.
  2. Library. Your working pile of pulled recordings. Pick one and hit Select. A recording stays here until you publish it, then it moves to History. If you ever delete it from History, it comes right back here.
  3. Transcribe. Hit Generate checklist and wait. This runs on your own PC, free, and turns your spoken callouts into a list. (This step needs no internet.)
  4. Review. Read the list, fix anything it misheard, and add or remove items by hand. This is your last chance to tidy it before it goes out.
  5. Publish. Fill in the Device and Procedure (use + New to save one for next time, 🗑️ to drop one you added by mistake), a Title, and optional Description and Notes. Choose which Vimeo folder it lands in. Publish uploads the video to Vimeo and posts the checklist card to the Teams channel.
  6. Jobs. Your upload queue. Each publish shows a live progress bar here and runs in the background, so you can line up the next one while it works. A green check and a Vimeo link mean it finished.
  7. History. Every review you've published, grouped by device. As the team knocks out fixes, tick the items off here to track progress (the count shows how many are done). Delete a review to clear it, and Fetch asks if you also want to delete its Vimeo video. The Teams message stays either way.
